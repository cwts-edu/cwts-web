# Doctor of Ministry (4-6 years, 40 credits hours)

The D.Min. program is designed to provide pastors holding an M.Div. (or its equivalent) an opportunity for further training through the guidance of experienced teachers and the mutual sharing of students, such that they may be spiritually vitalized and enabled to reflect on a deeper level the theory and practice of their pastoral ministry or other ministries, to appreciate and integrate the two better with fresh insight, and thus serve churches more effectively.

The program consists of 3 components, including required courses, elective courses and thesis-project.

- Required Courses: (2 courses, 4 credits) Research Methodology for D.Min. (4); Research Methods* (0). <br>
_*Research Methods is a required self-study course offered every semester and graded on a Pass/Fail basis (DMin passing score: 80% [B-]). The course fee is $96. CWTS master’s graduates who have previously taken this course must retake it if they did not achieve a score of 80% (B-) or higher._
- 7 courses (totaling 28 credits) are to be taken from the following four categories (preferably not more than 4 courses from one particular category) in Pastoral Counseling, Church Leadership, Preaching & Teaching, and Evangelism & Mission.
- The thesis / project (8 credits): must be directly related to the major of study, involve the integration of theory and praxis, demonstrate creativity and make a concrete contribution. It can take the form of an a church research, a training manual, a study guide, or a ministry project.
